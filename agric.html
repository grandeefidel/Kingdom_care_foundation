AGRICULTURAL EMPOWERMENT PROGRAMS

The kingdom Care Foundation creates batches of agricultural empowerment programs in many parts of Nigeria in order to create livelihood for the rural and suburban communities. We promote community-based on-farm and off-farm business activities along key agricultural value chain as a mechanism for job and wealth creation amongst unemployed masses in aforementioned households. This program helps to eradicate poverty and reduces the number of crimes in our communities.

AGRICULTURAL PROJECT DEVELOPMENT INCLUDES:

Snail farming (Heliciculture), Bee farming (Apiculture) and honey production, Fish farming, Piggery, Rabbitry, Poultry, Grass cutter farming, Mushroom cultivation, Termite farming, Butterfly farming & silk production.

SNAIL FARMING (HELICICULTURE)

This is the rearing or domestication of snails. The history of snail farming dates back to Romans who indeed farmed snails. In Africa, Snail farming is relatively new since large quantity of snails can easily be picked from the forests. Today, the depletion of natural forests as a result of logging, deforestation and bush burning, and the protracted search for cheap source of meat (protein), there has been urgent need for snail farming in the continent both at small scale and commercial quantities.

One good agricultural business that fall under meat production industry that a prospective entrepreneur can successfully start is snail farming. Snail farming business has a promising future and a lot of comparative advantages over other livestock such as poultry and fish farming. Apart from converting wastes into wealth, it creates job opportunities and employs massively as every products of snail-meat, slime (fluid), shell and feces are economically important and internationally acceptable!

What you need to know about Snail and Snail farming?

Snail is an invertebrate with soft body that is covered with hard calcareous shell. It belongs to the kingdom animalia, phylum mollusca, class gastropoda and order Pulmonata. It is a neglected animal but recently its medicinal and nutritional values have been recognized. Many species of terrestrial snails are edible, but the most populous species of economic interest in West Africa include: African Giant Snails (Archachatina marginata & Achatina achatina). They are highly fecund or reproductive because of their hermaphroditic nature. Their off-springs grow faster matures in 3-6 months if good weather & food materials are available. They are herbivorous in feeding habit. Each of the snails can lay 6-12 eggs in a day, up to 50-100 eggs in a month or 500-1000 eggs in a year, between the months of February – October (rainy season). Their eggs hatch in 2-4 weeks (14-34 days). Snails can grow & live up to 9 years. To that effect including the Rice and poultry projects, we have set aside about 1000 hectares of land located in the center of Asaba, Delta State, Nigeria.

Benefits of Snail Farming

Apart from being highly profitable and creating employment opportunities, it is cheaper and less tedious than the poultry and fish farming. Snail meat (escargot) is a special delicacy due to its medicinal value, high protein & iron contents, low fat & near zero cholesterol. Besides, the shells of snails have artistic value and also used in formulation of livestock feeds as calcium and phosphorus supplements. The excreta and remnants of food from snail farm is a cheap source of mature. Expected period of time for returns on investment in snail farming is very short (3-4 months). Again, massive increment in population of snails can be achieved in a short time within a limited (small) space. Little or no money is spent on feeding snails because their food is locally sourced. Snails are very easy and convenient to rear because they are slow in action, friendly and don’t make noise, bite, smell, or run about. Finally, snail farming has saved snails from extinction.

THE AIM OF THE PROJECT IS TO PARTNER WITH A GRANT DONOR TO SET UP SNAIL FARMING SETTLEMENTS WHERE HUNDREDS OF UNEMPLOYED YOUNG PEOPLE CAN BE EMPLOYED, TRAINED AND SUBSEQUENTLY EMPOWERED TO SET UP AND START THEIR OWN SNAIL FARMS AS APPLICABLE' This would be replicated over and over again to continuously impact on the livelihood of hundreds of youths.
